Dec 01, 2021

Staying humble after finding recognition.

Charlie Jackson has inspired many other artists with his inventive and curious approach to the landscape and human form. An Archibald Prize winner, educator and respected painter, his contribution to Canada’s visual culture has been enormous. He creates about his colourful life and career – the rainforest, the permutations of the art world, the primality of painting and the experience of growing older.

Taking the long historical view that this early reading allowed, Jackson's creative and other professional practices push back against many of the ideas bequeathed to us by socio-economic – if not always artistic – modernity.

Dec 03, 2021

Travelling the world inspires his work

Woodrow Finnegan is best known for his kaleidoscopic paintings and recently his sculptures, animations, and public art that employ traditional patterns with op-art sensibility, enlivened with glitter and dazzling colour. He was fresh out of art school when he left Auckland for Aviz, France, in 1997, to participate in the esteemed Moët & Chandon Arts Fellowship. Living on the outskirts of Paris in a crumbling chateau, he cycled to the station at any opportunity to journey to the city and visit the museums and galleries.

Dec 03, 2021

A mélange of contrasting tactile surfaces

The art of Selena Rosales could be described as a stylized medley of uplifting visual narratives and poetry in visual form. Her vibrant symbolic paintings evoke feelings of sensuality, love, unity, hope, fertility and eternity. In one series, nude male and female figures embrace each other enveloped in a graceful and continuous undulating design. In other paintings, figures dance among a joyful rhythmic display of flora and fauna and illuminate our symbiotic bond with nature.

Rosales' beautifully orchestrated compositions are enhanced in breadth and depth by her versatile use of colors, a mélange of contrasting tactile surfaces, and acute spatial perception. Stimulating elements of fantasy and reality are wedded in perfect harmony and balance.

Although at first glance Rosales' lyrical style may recall that of Symbolism, she expresses her own contemporary artistic vocabulary imbued with life-affirming metaphorical imagery.
